# Example: Suspension Chain — 4–3

## Input

Two voices in C major, 4–3 suspension chain.

## Output

Voice 1: E4 D4 C4 | G4 F4 E4
Voice 2: C4 C4 C4 | G4 G4 G4

## Notes

Beat 1: E4 (consonant with C). Beat 2: D4 over C4 — 4–3 suspension (D prepared by prior E? No — the preparation is the same pitch D held from a consonant moment). Standard 4–3: prepare D, suspend D over C, resolve D→C. The primitive operates by ensuring each dissonance is prepared and resolves down by step.
