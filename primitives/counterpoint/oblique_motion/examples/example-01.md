# Example: Oblique Motion — Suspension Setup

## Input

Two voices, preparing a 4–3 suspension.

## Output

Voice 1: D4 — D4 C4
Voice 2: G4 A4 B4

## Notes

Voice 1 sustains D4, then moves down to C4 (resolution). Voice 2 moves G→A→B. The oblique motion (Voice 1 static then resolving) creates the suspension. Useful for harmonic anchoring: one voice holds the chord tone while the other provides motion.
