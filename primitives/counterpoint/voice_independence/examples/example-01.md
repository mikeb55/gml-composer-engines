# Example: Voice Independence — Two Voices

## Input

Two voices in C major, 4 bars.

## Output

Voice 1: C4 E4 G4 E4 | D4 F4 A4 G4 | E4 G4 C5 B4 | A4 G4 F4 E4
Voice 2: G3 C4 E4 C4 | B3 D4 F4 E4 | C4 E4 G4 F4 | E4 D4 C4 B3

## Notes

Each voice moves with its own contour. Voice 1 ascends then descends; Voice 2 has a different rhythmic profile (e.g. sustained notes on beats where Voice 1 moves). No identical attack patterns. Harmonic meaning (C major, G7, C) emerges from the combination, not from one voice "chordal" and the other "melodic."
