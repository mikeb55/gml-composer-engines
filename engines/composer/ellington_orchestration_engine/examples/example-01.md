# Example: Ellington Orchestration Engine — 8-Bar Background

## Input

harmonic_context: Cm7 | F7 | BbMaj7 | BbMaj7 (4 bars, repeated)
melodic_material: Lead trumpet melody (C5–G5)
ensemble_spec: 5 trumpets, 4 trombones, 5 saxes, rhythm

## Parameters

register_preference: clear_separation
dialogue_bias: 0.6
voicing_spread_default: open

## Output

Register assignment:
- Melody → high band (lead trumpet)
- Background figures → mid band (2nd/3rd trumpets, or saxes)

Section timeline (call-response):
- Bars 1–2: Brass (staccato hits on chord tones)
- Bars 3–4: Reeds (sustained pads, open voicing)
- Bars 5–6: Brass (variation of bars 1–2)
- Bars 7–8: Reeds (build to tutti)

Voicings: Open 4-way for reeds (Cm7: C–Eb–G–Bb spread); closed for brass hits.

## Notes

Classic brass-reed dialogue. Register separation keeps melody clear. Open voicings in reeds avoid muddiness. Ellington-style section colour. Beatrice-relevant for big band arranging.
