# Example: Jimmy Wyble Engine — Pedal-Tone Technique

## Harmonic Context

Cmaj7 for 4 bars

## Output (Conceptual)

**Upper line:** E4 G4 A4 B4 | C5 B4 A4 G4 | E4 F#4 G4 A4 | G4 E4 C4 —

**Lower line:** G2 G2 G2 G2 | G2 G2 G2 G2 | G2 G2 G2 G2 | G2 G2 G2 G2

## How It Works

- **Pedal tone:** Lower voice holds G2 (root of C) throughout. pedal_tone_anchor primitive.
- **Upper voice moves:** Melody moves above the pedal. Contrary/oblique implied by upper motion alone.
- **Rhythmic displacement:** Upper attacks can be syncopated against the pedal pulse.
- **Implied harmony:** Cmaj7 from E–G, G–G, A–G dyads. Pedal anchors; upper provides colour.

## Notes

Wyble basic contrapuntal device. Lower holds or repeats; upper moves. Then lower may resume motion.
